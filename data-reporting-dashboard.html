Executive Overview

In an information-dense environment, raw data is often overwhelming. This 4-week self-guided project focused on the "last mile" of analytics: converting static datasets into dynamic, visual decision-support tools.

The objective was to build a centralized Operational Dashboard using Microsoft Power BI that provides real-time visibility into healthcare performance metrics — reducing the time required for management to identify trends and anomalies across departments, clinical quality, financial performance, and workforce.

The dataset underpinning the dashboard was engineered from first principles — seven interconnected tables, 20,130 daily rows, and 110 distinct fields across a full leap year. Rather than using a pre-packaged dataset, the data was constructed at daily granularity with realistic seasonal and weekly variation, department-specific baselines, and controlled noise that makes anomaly detection meaningful in practice. Dataset at a Glance

What the data covers

The dataset models a multi-department hospital across FY 2024. Each table answers a distinct operational question — designed to mirror the actual domains a hospital management team would interrogate.

  • Daily KPIs — 366 rows of hospital-wide pulse: admissions, bed occupancy, OR utilization, satisfaction, net margin
  • Department Daily — 8 departments × 366 days (2,928 rows): revenue, LOS, complications, margin, weekend vs weekday patterns
  • Physician Daily — 12 physicians × 366 days (4,392 rows): patients seen, on-time start, revenue, leave flags
  • Patient Outcomes Daily — 8 procedure categories × 366 days (2,928 rows): success rates, HAI rate, protocol adherence, PRO scores
  • Financial Daily — 8 departments × 366 days (2,928 rows): revenue by stream, EBITDA, denial rate, cost per patient
  • Equipment Daily — 10 high-value assets × 366 days (3,660 rows): utilization rate, downtime, maintenance cost
  • Staffing Daily — 8 departments × 366 days (2,928 rows): shift headcount, vacancy, agency usage, staff-patient ratio

Technical Architecture

1. Data Modeling & Structuring

The dataset follows a denormalised star structure — each of the seven tables shares Date and Department as join keys, enabling Power BI to cross-filter across all dimensions simultaneously. Built-in Day of Week, Week No., and Quarter fields make rolling aggregations trivial without additional DAX date tables.

Data integrity was verified at source: all values were engineered within published clinical benchmarks (CMS Hospital Compare, NHS England operational standards, AHRQ quality indicators), with controlled seasonal and growth variation baked in mathematically rather than randomly assigned. The totals row uses Excel SUM and AVERAGE formulas — confirmed zero errors on recalculation.

2. Visualization Strategy

The dashboard interface was designed with a focus on clarity and accuracy. Rather than cluttering the screen with unnecessary graphics, chart types were selected to match the analytical question each metric is answering:

  • Trend Analysis: Line and area charts for performance trajectories — bed occupancy, admissions, revenue — with daily resolution and 7-day rolling averages to smooth operational noise.
  • Departmental Comparison: Bar charts and heatmaps to surface which departments are outliers on margin, complications, or LOS.
  • Clinical Quality Matrix: Scatter plots pairing protocol adherence with outcome rates to identify the "high-adherence, poor-outcome" anomalies worth investigating.
  • KPI Cards: High-level "North Star" metrics positioned at the top for immediate executive visibility — total admissions, net margin, patient satisfaction, mortality rate.
  • Workforce Intelligence: Small-multiple charts tracking vacancy and turnover by department, allowing HR and operations to identify which teams are structurally under pressure.

Business Impact

The primary outcome was the translation of complex, raw operational data into management-ready summaries at multiple levels of the organisation — from department head to CFO to clinical quality lead.

Decision Support

By visualizing operational metrics across patient flow, financial performance, clinical quality, and workforce simultaneously, the dashboard reduced cognitive load and shifted the workflow from "deciphering spreadsheets" to strategic decision-making based on verified, contextualised insights.

Cross-Domain Visibility

The seven-table structure means a leader can move from "ICU readmission rate is rising" → "ICU staff vacancy rate is at 11%" → "ICU overtime hours are trending up on weekday night shifts" in a single dashboard session — connecting the clinical, operational, and workforce stories that are typically buried in separate systems.
