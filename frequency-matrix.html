Channel Performance Basis

Cadence decisions were not arbitrary — each channel's content frequency is proportional to its measured contribution in the GA4 dataset. The four channels below account for the vast majority of attributable session volume across the simulation period.

Data Source

Metric basis: Session share & conversion rate per channel across full period
Conv. rate: purchase events ÷ sessions per channel

Frequency Rationale

The decisions behind cadence — not just the cadence itself.

Saturation Principle

The frequency matrix is deliberately asymmetric. Each channel's cadence reflects a different strategic logic:

  • Organic Search — highest sustained cadence. SEO compounds: content published in Phase 1 continues generating sessions in Phases 2 and 3 at no incremental cost.
  • Paid Search — sharp Phase 2 peak, near-zero in Phase 3. Purchase volume data drives this directly: 833 conversions in peak week → 170 in cool-down. CPC spend follows conversion opportunity, not habit.
  • Email — 3× in Phase 2, fortnightly in Phase 3. High send frequency during peak is justified by purchase urgency. It drops sharply post-holiday to protect list health and open rates ahead of the next campaign.
  • Referral — consistent medium cadence across all phases. Partner relationships require ongoing communication regardless of season; one touchpoint per week maintains momentum without overcommitting production resources.
