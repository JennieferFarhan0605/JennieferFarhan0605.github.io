Executive Summary

To bridge the gap between theoretical marketing concepts and operational reality, I executed a comprehensive 8-week self-guided simulation designed to replicate the pressure of a live digital marketing environment. This project moved beyond simple content creation to focus on the analytical infrastructure required to track success.

The core objective was to design a full-cycle campaign—from audience identification to post-mortem analysis—using structured datasets to practice data-driven decision making and optimization through a full-funnel lens.

Evidence-Backed Approach

Conversion and revenue signals were derived from Google’s public GA4 BigQuery sample ecommerce export, reshaped into daily channel-level reporting outputs. The dataset build process, query validation, and export workflow are documented end-to-end in the companion dataset page.

Strategic Framework

1. Audience Segmentation & Targeting

Effective campaigns begin with precision targeting. Rather than broadcasting broadly and hoping for traction, I built a structured audience segmentation model to ensure every message reached the right person at the right stage of their decision journey. This sharpened the analytical signal downstream—when performance data came in, it could be evaluated against who was targeted, not just what was spent.

Demographic Profiling

I defined core audience segments across age brackets, geographic focus areas, and professional status indicators. Each segment was mapped against product-fit criteria to determine where value alignment was strongest—concentrating acquisition efforts on users most likely to convert, not simply those easiest to reach.

Psychographic Mapping

Beyond demographics, I layered in motivational and pain-point analysis to understand the underlying drivers behind purchase decisions. This informed the messaging hierarchy: leading with the outcome a user wants, rather than the product feature being sold. Psychographic clarity is what separates content that earns a click from content that gets scrolled past.

Channel Strategy

Each segment was matched to the acquisition channel most likely to intercept them at the right intent signal. This channel-to-segment mapping prevented budget from being spread uniformly and instead concentrated effort where each specific audience profile was most active and receptive—a foundation that made later conversion analysis meaningful rather than aggregate guesswork.

2. Content & Operational Planning

Audience clarity is only half the equation. I translated segmentation outputs into a structured content calendar designed to sustain a consistent brand narrative across a multi-channel environment—without creating production bottlenecks or audience fatigue.

Frequency Matrix

A common content planning failure is treating all assets as equal in cadence. I designed a tiered frequency model that separated high-volume, short-form formats (reactive, high-frequency) from lower-cadence, high-investment assets (long-form, evergreen, authority-building). This ensured the brand maintained consistent visibility throughout the campaign window without saturating the audience or diluting the impact of key pieces.

Asset Coordination

All deliverables were organized by format type, channel applicability, production lead time, and technical specification requirements. This asset coordination layer created a single operational view of what needed to be produced, in what format, and by when—eliminating last-minute scrambles and ensuring no channel went dark due to missing creative.

Framework Output

The combined segmentation model and content plan provided the strategic brief that downstream analytics could measure against. Performance data could be evaluated in the context of who was targeted, through which channel, and with which content type—enabling attribution-level insight rather than surface-level volume reporting.

Data Infrastructure & Analysis

The backbone of this project was a structured reporting model designed to mimic real-world performance tracking. Raw outputs were shaped into channel-level reporting tables to support trend analysis, funnel visibility, and KPI monitoring.

Excel Dashboard Architecture

I built Excel trackers to monitor campaign health through a full-funnel lens. This required spreadsheet modeling to translate raw rows into decision-ready KPIs and summaries.

  • Top-of-Funnel (Sessions): Tracked daily sessions by channel to measure acquisition and awareness.
  • Conversion Signals (Purchases): Captured purchase events as conversion outcomes.
  • Value Tracking (Revenue): Used revenue as a quality signal to measure channel contribution beyond volume.
  • Funnel KPI: Computed a conversion rate per session to identify performance differences across channels.

Analysis Notes

This version is deliberately structured around GA4-derived funnel signals (sessions → purchases → revenue). In a next iteration, delivery metrics (impressions, clicks, cost) can be added as a paid-media table to compute CTR, CPA, and ROAS.

Insights & Optimization

Data without action is overhead. The project concluded with a structured performance report containing insights and optimization recommendations based on channel contribution and conversion efficiency.

Key Findings

The analysis highlighted that high acquisition volume does not necessarily translate to efficient conversion. By comparing daily sessions against purchase outcomes at channel level, I identified performance differences in conversion efficiency and recommended reallocating effort toward the channels and content formats that showed stronger conversion and value contribution.
