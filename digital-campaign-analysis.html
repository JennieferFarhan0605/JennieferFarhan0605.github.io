Executive Summary

To bridge the gap between theoretical marketing concepts and operational reality, I executed a comprehensive 8-week self-guided simulation designed to replicate the pressure of a live digital marketing environment. This project moved beyond simple content creation to focus on the analytical infrastructure required to track success.

The core objective was to design a full-cycle campaign—from audience identification to post-mortem analysis—using simulated datasets to practice data-driven decision making and optimization.

Strategic Framework

1. Audience Segmentation & Targeting

Effective campaigns begin with precision targeting. I developed detailed user personas and segmentation strategies to ensure resource efficiency.

  • Demographic Profiling: Defined core audience segments based on age, location, and professional status to align with product fit.
  • Psychographic Mapping: Analyzed potential pain points and motivations to tailor messaging that resonates with specific user needs.
  • Channel Strategy: Mapped specific segments to the most effective acquisition channels, reducing hypothetical wasted ad spend.

2. Content & Operational Planning

I structured a comprehensive content calendar to maintain a consistent brand narrative across a multi-channel environment.

  • Frequency Matrix: Balanced high-frequency "snackable" content with lower-frequency, high-value assets to maintain engagement without saturation.
  • Asset Coordination: Organized deliverables by format (video, static, carousel) and platform requirements to streamline the hypothetical production workflow.

Data Infrastructure & Analysis

The backbone of this project was the development of a robust tracking system. I simulated campaign performance using sample datasets to test my ability to interpret raw metrics.

Excel Dashboard Architecture

I built Excel trackers to monitor the health of the campaign through a full-funnel lens. This required advanced spreadsheet modeling to translate raw data rows into actionable intelligence.

  • Top-of-Funnel Metrics (Reach): Monitored impression volume to assess brand awareness and market penetration.
  • Mid-Funnel Engagement (CTR): Analyzed Click-Through Rates to evaluate creative resonance and ad copy effectiveness. Low CTRs were flagged as indicators of creative fatigue or poor audience alignment.
  • Bottom-of-Funnel (Conversions): Tracked final conversion events to calculate the hypothetical Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) and overall campaign ROI.

Insights & Optimization

Data without action is overhead. The project concluded with the production of a performance report containing specific insights and optimization recommendations.

Key Findings

The analysis highlighted that while broad targeting delivered high Reach, it suffered from low conversion efficiency. By analyzing the data, I determined that adjusting content types based on early CTR data—specifically shifting budget toward high-engagement video assets—could significantly improve the overall campaign ROI.
